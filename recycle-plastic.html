10 Creative Ways to Recycle Plastic

1) Coffee Creamer Containers for Snack Storage

Looking for a creative way to recycle your plastic coffee creamer bottles?
Why not try filling the old containers with your favourite small snacks for the easy ability to pour! Snacks like nuts, candies, or even cereal can be stored in these containers.



2) Plastic Bottle Planter

Looking to start working on your green thumb?
Or do you just want to have access to fresh herbs or other small plants? Why not upcycle your old plastic bottles into small planters. These planters can be used to grow a variety of plants and are perfect for small spaces.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Cut out the bottom third of a 2-liter bottle.
  2. Paint the bottle white or the color of your choice.
  3. Fill the bottle with seeds and soil



3) Herb Garden With Empty 2-Liter Bottles:

If a small planter isn’t your style why not upcycle your empty 2-litre bottles into a herb garden. You can follow these steps to create your own herb garden with six empty bottles. This is a great way to have fresh herbs at your fingertips.

    Follow these teps:

  1. Remove the label from the bottle and clean the inside.
  2. Take a sharp object and poke drainage holes in the top third of the bottle.
  3. Poke a hole in the side of the bottle for hanging.



4) Beach Bucket From Laundry Detergent Containers

Got a pileup of old laundry detergent bottles? Save them for your next trip to the beach by turning them into a new beach bucket! Simply cut the bottom third of the laundry detergent bottle to create a sturdy bucket for your beach adventures.



5) Soda Bottles by Creating a Vertical Garden

For those wanting to double down and create their own private vertical garden, this is perfect for you. Plastic bottles are perfect for those needing storage for a variety of plants both indoors and outdoors.



6) DIY Sprinkler

Why not turn your old plastic bottle into a new way to water your garden or lawn? Most 2-L bottles can attach to the common end of an outdoor hose so it’s easier than you think! All you need to do is poke holes in the bottle to create a sprinkler effect.



7) Piggy Bank Made From a Reused Plastic Bottle

Looking to save some money for your next special purchase? Why not turn your old plastic bottle into an upcycled piggy bank to store your change. Join us now and upcycle a plastic bottle to create a fun and practical piggy bank.



8) Floral spring lights

Revamp your home with these beautiful spring lights. You don’t need much skills for this. Simply cut and paint various shapes of flowers from bottles (with their caps attached) to fit a string of lights. These lights will add a touch of spring to any room.



9) No-sew zipper stationery case

You will need two 20 oz. soda bottles, a zipper, and hot glue for this stationery case. Cut the bottom of one bottle and the top of another. Use hot glue to glue the zipper to both ends of the bottles, and you have a handy no-sew zipper stationery case.



10) Sink extender

DIY faucet extenders of any design or colour for your kids using empty lotion or shampoo bottles and spray paint. Remember to wash the bottles clean and just cut out the shape of the extender to fit your faucet for easy use.
