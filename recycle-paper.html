10 Creative Ways to Recycle Paper

1) DIY Basket Paper

Did you know that you can make a two colorful, handmade baskets crafted from rolled paper tubes woven together?

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper or colored paper into thin tubes and glue the ends.
  2. Cut a circular or oval cardboard piece as the basket's base.
  3. Glue vertical paper tubes around the edge of the base.
  4. Weave additional tubes in and out of the vertical ones to form the basket's sides.
  5. Trim excess tubes, glue the ends, and paint the basket in your desired colors.




2) DIY Spherical Lamp

Did you know that with just a small circular materials, possibly paper rolls or plastic rings, with a glowing light inside, you can already make your own lamp.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Collect and cut small rings from paper rolls.
  2. Glue the rings together in a spherical shape, leaving space for a light bulb.
  3. Fix the sphere onto a sturdy base.
  4. Place an LED bulb or fairy lights inside the sphere.
  5. Ensure all parts are firm, then turn on the light for a glowing effect.

4) DIY Organizer

Did you know that with just a newspaper tubes, and featured compartments you already have your own small organizer.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper into thin tubes and secure with glue.
  2. Cut and shape cardboard pieces to form the base and compartments.
  3. Use paper tubes to weave around the frame for a basket-like effect.
  4. Make a small box from cardboard, cover it with paper tubes, and attach a handle.
  5. Glue all parts firmly and paint or varnish for a finished look.




5) DIY Wind Chime or Hanging Decoration

Did you know that with just a recycled newspaper, featuring multiple layers of paper cones and rings, you already have your own wind decor.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper into thin tubes and shape some into cones.
  2. Cut a sturdy circular cardboard or use woven paper tubes as the top frame.
  3. Suspend paper cones and rings using thread or string from different levels.
  4. Arrange and glue each layer properly for a cascading effect.
  5. Paint, add beads, and ensure all parts are firmly attached.

7) DIY Candy Bowl

Did you know that with just a shaped paper pieces, arranged in a lace-like pattern, you already have your own candy bowl.

  3. Apply a layer of glue or varnish to strengthen the bowl.
  4. Adjust the top edge for a smooth or decorative look.
  5. Once dry, paint or leave it natural for a unique effect.
