10 Creative Ways to Recycle Paper

1) DIY Basket Paper

Did you know that you can make two colorful, handmade baskets crafted from rolled paper tubes woven together? These baskets are perfect for storing small items and add a touch of creativity to your home decor.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper or colored paper into thin tubes and glue the ends.
  2. Cut a circular or oval cardboard piece as the basket's base.
  3. Glue vertical paper tubes around the edge of the base.




2) DIY Spherical Lamp

Did you know that with just small circular materials, possibly paper rolls or plastic rings, with a glowing light inside, you can already make your own lamp? This lamp is not only functional but also adds a unique touch to any room.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Collect and cut small rings from paper rolls.
  2. Glue the rings together in a spherical shape, leaving space for a light bulb.
  3. Fix the sphere onto a sturdy base.


3) DIY Woven Basket with a Handle

Did you know that with just rolled paper tubes and decorated with paper roses, you already have your own basket? This basket is perfect for storing small items and adds a touch of elegance to your home.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper or colored paper into thin tubes and glue the ends.
  2. Cut a round cardboard piece and attach vertical paper tubes around it.
  3. Weave more tubes in and out to create the basket shape.




4) DIY Organizer

Did you know that with just newspaper tubes and featured compartments, you already have your own small organizer? This organizer is perfect for storing stationery and other small items.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper into thin tubes and secure with glue.
  2. Cut and shape cardboard pieces to form the base and compartments.
  3. Use paper tubes to weave around the frame to create the organizer.




5) DIY Wind Chime or Hanging Decoration

Did you know that with just recycled newspaper, featuring multiple layers of paper cones and rings, you already have your own wind decor? This wind chime adds a touch of creativity and sound to your outdoor space.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper into thin tubes and shape some into cones.
  2. Cut a sturdy circular cardboard or use woven paper tubes as the top frame.
  3. Suspend paper cones and rings using string or wire to create the wind chime.




6) DIY Heart-shaped Jewelry Box

Did you know that with just rolled newspaper tubes, decorated with small paper quilled designs, you already have your own jewelry box? This box is perfect for storing jewelry and adds a touch of elegance to your vanity.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper strips into thin tubes and glue the ends.
  2. Cut a heart-shaped cardboard piece and attach vertical paper tubes around it.
  3. Use more tubes to weave around the frame to create the box.


7) DIY Candy Bowl

Did you know that with just shaped paper pieces, arranged in a lace-like pattern, you already have your own candy bowl? This bowl is perfect for storing candies and adds a touch of creativity to your home decor.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll strips of paper tightly into small circular or oval shapes.
  2. Arrange and glue the paper rolls in a bowl-like form using a mold or freehand.
  3. Apply a layer of varnish or glue to secure the shape.




8) DIY Desk Organizer

Did you know that with just woven newspaper tubes, designed with compartments and drawers for storing stationery and small items, you already have your own desk organizer? This organizer is perfect for keeping your desk tidy and adds a touch of creativity to your workspace.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper into thin tubes and glue the ends.
  2. Cut and assemble cardboard pieces to form the base and compartments.
  3. Use paper tubes to weave around the frame for added strength.




9) DIY Pen Organizer with Cellphone Holder

Did you know that with just rolled newspaper tubes, you already have your own cellphone holder with a pen organizer? This organizer is perfect for keeping your phone and pens in one place and adds a touch of creativity to your desk.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll newspaper strips tightly and glue the ends.
  2. Attach several paper tubes together to form a sturdy base.
  3. Arrange and glue tubes to shape a phone stand and a pen holder.




10) DIY Hexagonal Organizer

Did you know that with just woven newspaper tubes, you already have your own hexagonal organizer? This organizer is perfect for storing small items and adds a touch of creativity to your home.

    Follow these simple steps:

  1. Roll and glue newspaper strips into tubes.
  2. Arrange and glue tubes into a hexagonal shape.
  3. Weave tubes vertically and secure them with glue.
  4. Attach additional tubes to create compartments if desired.
